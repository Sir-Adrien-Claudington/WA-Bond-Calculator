WA Tenant Bond Refund Guide

Know your rights under the Residential Tenancies Act 1987 (WA)

When you move out of a rental property in Western Australia, you are entitled to have your bond returned — minus any legitimate deductions the landlord can prove. This guide explains exactly what landlords can and cannot take from your bond, and what to do if you disagree with their claim.

What is a rental bond?

A rental bond is a security deposit, typically four weeks’ rent, held by the WA Bond Administrator during your tenancy. It does not belong to the landlord — it is your money, held in trust. The landlord must lodge it within 14 days of receiving it under the Residential Tenancies Act 1987 (WA) s 29.

What can a landlord deduct?

Landlords can only deduct from the bond for:

  • Rent you owe at the end of the tenancy
  • Cleaning beyond the original condition of the property
  • Damage you caused — beyond fair wear and tear
  • Replacement of keys, remotes, or access devices you did not return
  • Fumigation or pet-related damage if a pet bond was charged (capped at $350)

What a landlord cannot deduct

A landlord cannot deduct for fair wear and tear — the natural ageing of the property through normal use. If the carpet was already worn, the walls already marked, or the paint already fading when you moved in, the landlord cannot charge you for it now. See our fair wear and tear guide for examples.

How to get your bond back — Form 6

At the end of the tenancy, the landlord or tenant lodges Form 6 (Application for Disposal of Security Bond) with the Bond Administrator. If both parties agree on the split, the process is straightforward. If you disagree with the landlord’s proposed deductions, do not sign a Form 6 you are not happy with.

How to dispute a bond claim

If you cannot agree with your landlord, either party can apply to the State Administrative Tribunal of Western Australia (SAT) for a binding decision. SAT hearings are relatively informal and do not require legal representation. You can also contact Consumer Protection WA for free advice before lodging an application.

To prepare for a dispute, gather your evidence: the ingoing condition report, outgoing condition report, photos from both inspections, and any communications with your landlord.
