WA Landlord Rental Bond Claim Guide

Under the Residential Tenancies Act 1987 (WA) — updated for the 2024 amendments

As a landlord in Western Australia, you can claim against a tenant’s security bond at the end of a tenancy — but only for specific, evidenced costs. This guide explains exactly what you can and cannot claim, how to apply, and what happens if the tenant disputes your claim.

What you can claim from the bond

Under the Residential Tenancies Act 1987 (WA), a landlord may only claim for:

  • Unpaid rent — any rent owed at the end of the tenancy
  • Cleaning — costs to bring the property back to its original clean condition (not better than it was at the start)
  • Damage beyond fair wear and tear — deliberate or negligent damage, burns, stains, holes, broken fixtures
  • Replacement of keys, remotes, or access devices — if not returned at the end of the tenancy
  • Pet bond costs — fumigation or pet-related damage only, up to $350 total

What you cannot claim

You cannot claim for fair wear and tear — the natural deterioration of a property through ordinary, reasonable use. This includes carpet worn in walkways, minor scuffs on walls, and paint faded by sunlight. See our fair wear and tear guide for a full list of examples.

Maximum bond amount

The maximum bond you can require is four weeks’ rent under s 29 of the Residential Tenancies Act 1987 (WA). You must lodge the bond with the WA Bond Administrator within 14 days of receiving it.

How to claim — Form 6

To claim all or part of the bond, lodge Form 6 (Application for Disposal of Security Bond) with the Bond Administrator. You will need to provide evidence for every deduction you claim, including:

  • Ingoing and outgoing condition reports
  • Photographs from the start and end of the tenancy
  • Invoices or quotes for cleaning and repairs
  • Rent ledger showing any arrears

The tenant has the right to agree or dispute your claim. If you cannot reach agreement, either party can apply to the State Administrative Tribunal of Western Australia (SAT) for a binding decision.

What if your claim exceeds the bond?

If your total claim is greater than the bond held, you can only recover the bond amount through Form 6. To recover the excess, you must make a separate application to SAT. The bond cannot be stretched beyond its total value.
